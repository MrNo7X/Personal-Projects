Summary about Sabotage by Neal Bascomb!

Find out more about Operation Gunnerside

Sabotage is a book about Operation Gunnerside, an operation aimed to destroy the Norsk heavy water plant at Vemork, and later sinking of the SF Hydro. On April 9, 1940, Germany invaded Denmark and Norway, aimed to capture the ports of Norway, and its precious iron ore, but another secret reason was to capture the heavy water facility at Vemork. Heavy water, or deuterium oxide was discovered as a better substitute as a moderator to slow down neutrons to promote nuclear fission without absorbing a significant amount of neutrons. The hydropower plant in Vemork was the only large scale producer of heavy water at the time, and after proving that heavy water works, the Germans seized the plant to produce more heavy water so that they could build a prototype atomic bomb. The British and Americans were also developing the atomic bomb under the codename Tube Alloys and Manhattan Project. British SIS(Secret Intelligence Service) became aware of the involvement of Vemork in the creation of the bomb in 1941 by an Norweigian plant manager who had fled Norway. In 1942. a 4 person team of SOE(Special Operation Executive) trained Norweigian commandos parachuted in Norway in an effort to destroy the plant(Grouse). While they skied cross country, they met up with a local resistance fighter and stayed in cabins while using minimal supplies. After a failed operation in late 1942 (Freshman), the team ran critically low on supplies and was forced to hunt for animals in the Norweigian winter. In 1943, after the harsh Norweigian winter, the team came out of hiding from the wilderness and made their way to the plant. They were later joined by an additional 6 commandos (Gunnerside) and made the final preparation for the assault. They eventually destroyed the electrolysis chambers and escaped without alarming the Germans, 100% success. Later, after each of them had gone different ways to escape from the German SS, one of them organized another sabotage that sunk the SF Hydro that was carrying some heavy water. This book describes the journey of these passionate Norweigians and the hardships they endured. If they had not destroyed the electrolysis chambers, Germany could have created a working atomic bomb that would change the tide of the war forever.

Dedicated to all men and women who fought on both sides of the war.
